The Importance of Hand-washing¶

Dr. Ignaz Semmelweis was a Hungarian physican and has been referred to as the "saviour of mothers". This is because, in the mid 19th century, Puerperal fever (also known as "childbed fever") was a common and fatal disease in hospitals. Dr. Semmelweis discovered that this fever was caused by doctors transferring "poison" (now known as hemolytic streptococcus) from corpses in the autopsy rooms to the women in the delivery rooms. He understood that disinfecting hands could decrease the transmission of this poison, and so he proposed the practice of washing hands with chlorinated lime solutions. His observations conflicted with the established medical opinions of the time, and he could not offer acceptable scientific explainations for his findings. His idea was rejected by the medical community as many of the docters were outraged at the suggestion that they were responsible for spreading this fever and causing their patients' deaths. They mocked him and in 1865, Dr. Semmelweis suffered a nervous breakkdown and was comitted to an asylum by his colleagues. He died two weeks later.

Clinic 1¶

Note from the above figure that the yearly proportion of deaths in Clinic 1 is much higher than in Clinic 2. There was one difference between the two clinics: medical students worked in Clinic 1, while midwives students worked in Clinic 2. Midwives exclusively assisted women giving birth, but the medical students were also performing autopsies. Dr. Semmelweis hypothesized that the medical students are spreading something from the corpses, and that this was causing an alarming amount of women to die. In the summer of 1847 (1847-06-01), Dr. Semmelweis decided to make washing hands mandatory which was a controversial decision at the time. We will now examine monthly data from Clinic 1 to see if his decision had an effect on the proprotion of deaths.

# Date when handwashing was made mandatory
handwashing_start = pd.to_datetime('1847-06-01')

# Split monthly into before and after handwashing_start
before_washing = monthly[monthly["date"] < handwashing_start]
after_washing = monthly[monthly["date"] >= handwashing_start]

# Plot monthly proportion of deaths before and after handwashing
ax = before_washing.plot(x="date", y="proportion_deaths",
                         label="Before handwashing")
after_washing.plot(x="date", y="proportion_deaths",
                   label="After handwashing", ax=ax, color='green')
ax.set_title('Proportion of Deaths in Clinic 1 of the Vienna General Hospital')
ax.set_ylabel("Proportion deaths")

Conclusion¶

The figures shows that hand-washing had a huge effect on the proportion of deaths, and as the calculations above show, hand-washing reduced the monthly proportion of deaths by 8 percentage points. Therefore, wash your hands.
